Set Your Sample Rate
--------------------

This step is **so often** overlooked, and can cause real headaches further down the line. Particularly if you‚Äôre sending your work to a video editor to do the final synchronisation.

Basically, the ‚Äúsample rate‚Äù is the number of times the audio is ‚Äúlooked at‚Äù by the computer per second. For **music** the default is 44.1kHz (that‚Äôs 44,100 times per second ‚Äì and how Logic automatically opens projects) but for **film audio** its 48kHz (48,000 times per second). All those moments in the movie that you worked so hard to emphasise aren‚Äôt going to line up.

Go to ‚ÄúFile‚Äù > ‚ÄúProject Settings‚Äù > ‚ÄúAudio‚Äù

[![logic pro x](https://unlockyoursound.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/3-Project-Settings-1024x983.png)](https://unlockyoursound.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/3-Project-Settings.png)

And change the ‚ÄúSample Rate‚Äù to 48kHz

[![logic pro x](https://unlockyoursound.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/4-48kHz-1024x404.png)](https://unlockyoursound.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/4-48kHz.png)

From here onwards is where personal preference starts to kick in. You might want to do things in a different order to this, and that‚Äôs totally fine and up to you.

The first thing I‚Äôd recommend you do is set up some markers so that you have an overview of the movie. If you‚Äôre working with a director, you might have already discussed this in a ‚ÄúSpotting Session.‚Äù

Open your marker window by clicking on the three lines at the top-right of the screen, and then selecting ‚ÄúMarker‚Äù

As we don‚Äôt know what tempos we‚Äôll be using yet, we don‚Äôt want to set the markers to ‚Äúbars‚Äù or beats, as they‚Äôll be moving all over the place when we start playing around with tempo.

Therefore you‚Äôll want to go to ‚ÄúView‚Äù > ‚ÄúShow Event Position and Length as Time‚Äù

[![logic pro x](https://unlockyoursound.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/9-Position-and-Length-as-Time.png)](https://unlockyoursound.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/9-Position-and-Length-as-Time.png)

Then set you playhead to ‚Äú0‚Äù (hit the ‚Äúreturn‚Äù key) and click on the ‚Äú+‚Äù sign to add your first marker. Watch your movie through and adjust the length of the marker to match each ‚Äúsection‚Äù of the piece you‚Äôre going to write. For example, your movie might start with a romantic section before moving into something more funny. Set your first marker to cover the whole of the romantic section and then add a second marker (by placing your playhead and using the ‚Äú+‚Äù button in the ‚ÄúMarker‚Äù tab) to cover your ‚Äúfunny‚Äù section.

Tempo & Signature
-----------------

Next to the ‚ÄúMarker‚Äù section you‚Äôll see options for ‚ÄúTempo‚Äù and ‚ÄúSignature‚Äù

[![logic pro x](https://unlockyoursound.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/10-Tempo-and-Signature.png)](https://unlockyoursound.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/10-Tempo-and-Signature.png)

This is where writing to picture really changes from (most) songwriting projects. We aren‚Äôt going to set the tempo/signature for the **whole project** and leave it at that. We‚Äôll be including a variety of tempo and signature changes throughout the piece.

You should already have an idea of what kind of piece you‚Äôll be writing, and will therefore know what signature you‚Äôll be writing in. Go ahead and set that as the main project signature for now. This is the only time you‚Äôll touch that ‚Äúmain‚Äù signature section.

I always say the best way to set your tempo is to **feel it**. Don‚Äôt try to work it out mathematically or anything like that, as it will always feel a bit off. Watch your movie, with your melody in mind, and try to figure out a natural pulse. Then you can use a [BPM counter](https://www.all8.com/tools/bpm.htm) to tap along and figure out what your tempo is. Go ahead and set that as well, to whatever showed up on the BPM counter.

Now we just need to tweak things to make sure everything lines up perfectly. There are two things you might need to adjust here:

1. The tempo (by very slight amounts)
2. The signature (to add/remove a beat)

If you can get away with just adjusting the tempo, great. Adding/removing a beat can be **really** difficult to get sounding natural in a composition.

Move your playhead to the end of the section (where your marker ends) and then put in on the **closest** full beat. With a bit of luck, this will be a barline. If it‚Äôs not, try adjusting your tempo by 1 or 2 bpm in either direction to see if that makes the barline line up. If it still hasn‚Äôt, then we‚Äôll have to mess around with signatures, but first, let‚Äôs make sure our tempo is perfect.

Make sure your playhead is on a barline **or** beat and then you simply have to drag the tempo numbers **after** the decimal point (by default these are .0000) up or down while watching the movie closely. As you drag the numbers up you‚Äôll see the movie moving backwards, and as you drag them down you‚Äôll see the movie progress. Drag them up/down until your beat/barline falls exactly on the cut or image that you want your music to hit. Do that for **every** decimal point and your music will hit the point at the **exact moment**. To be honest, being that precise is overkill, but can be good practice.

Now, if your scene cut didn‚Äôt line up perfectly with a barline, you‚Äôll need to adjust the signature at a point too. To do so, you‚Äôll probably want to start some composing first, so that you can work out **where** you‚Äôre going to add/remove a beat. Once you know, just line up your marker to the correct bar, click on the ‚Äúsignature‚Äù window, and click the ‚Äú+‚Äù icon, adjust the signature, then move your playhead to the **next** bar, and set the signature back to what it way. Simples.

Going forward, you‚Äôll make any signature or tempo changes in those windows. **Do not touch** the ‚Äúproject‚Äù tempo in the bar at the top centre of the screen, as it‚Äôll **ruin everything!!!**

Compose

The easy bit, right? Write your masterpiece! Telling how to compose the whole project is far beyond the scope of this article, but you‚Äôre set up and ready to go now, so this is the time to do that. A quick overview of the process I‚Äôd suggest going through though:
